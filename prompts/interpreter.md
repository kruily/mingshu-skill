# Technique Interpretation Template

## Purpose
Generate clear, accessible interpretations of classical Chinese 命书 text for matched techniques. This template ensures reverent yet accessible renditions that preserve the wisdom while making it actionable for modern readers.

## Tone Guidelines

### 风格要求
- **Reverent but accessible**: Honor the classical source while speaking in plain modern Chinese
- **Wise but not preachy**: Share insight without moralizing or lecturing
- **Practical over mystical**: Ground wisdom in actionable life guidance
- **Concise explanations**: Prefer clarity over elaborate paraphrase

### 翻译原则
- 原文应尽量保留古典韵味
- 白话解读要通俗易懂，但不俗气
- English should feel natural, not stilted
- Category should be a clean thematic label

## Template Structure

```markdown
### 命书第{N}术 · {Technique_Name}

**原文：**
{classical_text_exact}

**白话解读：**
{plain_modern_chinese_explanation}

**English Translation：**
{natural_english_rendition}

**Category：** {theme_category}

**Cultural Background：**
{brief_context_1-2_sentences_on_philosophy_or_origin}
```

## Section Definitions

### 原文 (Original Classical Text)
- Extract the exact classical Chinese passage from the source material
- Preserve punctuation as-is from original
- If technique has sub-parts (其一/其二), clearly indicate

### 白话解读 (Plain Modern Chinese)
- Translate the classical text into everyday modern Chinese
- Aim for how a wise but practical person would explain it to a friend
- Break down complex metaphors into concrete imagery
- 2-4 sentences typically sufficient
- Avoid: overly literary language, buzzwords, fortune-telling mysticism

### English Translation
- Render the modern Chinese interpretation in natural English
- Should read as original English prose, not translation
- Maintain the practical, grounded tone
- 1-3 sentences typically sufficient

### Category (Theme)
- Choose from established categories based on primary theme:
  - 环境健康 (Environment & Health)
  - 言行修养 (Speech & Conduct)
  - 财富之道 (Wealth)
  - 做事智慧 (Timing & Action)
  - 处世哲学 (Worldly Wisdom)
  - 人际之道 (Interpersonal)
- If technique spans multiple categories, pick the most relevant primary one

### Cultural Background
- 1-2 sentences explaining relevant philosophical context
- Can include: reference to 道家/儒家 concepts, classical allusions mentioned in text, historical context
- Purpose: helps readers understand why this wisdom developed
- Keep brief and illuminating, not encyclopedic

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing an interpretation, verify:

- [ ] 原文 accurately transcribed from source
- [ ] 白话解读 is in plain modern Chinese (no 文言文残留)
- [ ] English reads naturally, not like a translation
- [ ] Core insight is preserved and highlighted
- [ ] Practical application is discernible
- [ ] Tone is wise but accessible, not preachy or mystical
- [ ] Category accurately reflects primary theme
- [ ] Cultural Background adds context without over-explaining

## Example

### 命书第四术 · 取财有道

**原文：**
以见利之明处世，财可求也。以侵害之心得财，悖逆天道，必遭反报：非数倍偿债，即气运消减，身心俱伤。

**白话解读：**
只要眼光敏锐、洞察先机，财富是可以追求的。通过侵害别人的利益来获得财富，是违背天道的，必然会遭到反噬和报应——不是加倍还债，就是气运衰退，身心都会受到伤害。

**English Translation：**
Wealth can be pursued through sharp discernment of opportunities. But gaining wealth through harming others defies cosmic order and will bring retribution—either manifold debts or diminished fortune and harm to body and spirit.

**Category：** 财富之道 (Wealth)

**Cultural Background：**
This principle reflects the Confucian concept of "君子爱财，取之有道" (A noble person loves wealth but acquires it righteously), rooted in the belief that cosmic order (天道) naturally balances moral action with material outcome.

### 命书第七术 · 不贪之境

**原文：**
不贪者，贪之上境也。

**白话解读：**
不贪婪，才是贪婪的最高境界。这句话看似矛盾，实则蕴含深意：真正的智者不是没有欲望，而是懂得控制欲望，不被贪念所驱动。

**English Translation：**
Not being greedy is the highest form of restraint. This paradoxical wisdom suggests that true wisdom lies not in having no desires, but in mastering them.

**Category：** 处世哲学 (Worldly Wisdom)

**Cultural Background：**
This reflects Daoist teachings on wu-wei (无为) and Buddhist insights into attachment. The paradox points to a core truth: the person who truly has enough is one who has transcended the need to acquire.

---

### 命书第四十一术（其二）· 修身为本

**原文：**
逐成者，众庶之所趋；务修者，达人之所怀。怀修之人，是谓成矣。

**白话解读：**
追求成就，谁都在干；致力于修养，通达的人才看重。心怀修养的人，这才叫真正的成就。

**English Translation：**
Everyone chases success, but only the wise pursue self-cultivation. Those who embrace growth have achieved true accomplishment.

**Category：** 做事智慧 (Timing & Action)

**Cultural Background：**
This echoes Confucian teaching that self-cultivation is the foundation of all achievement. External success without internal refinement is hollow—the 达人 (accomplished person) prioritizes becoming over having.

## Notes on Multi-Part Techniques

Some techniques (like 第六术, 第四十一术) have distinct sub-parts marked 其一/其二. When interpreting these:
- Treat each sub-part as a separate interpretation unit
- Maintain the same structure for each
- Note the sub-part designation in the heading
- Each sub-part may have different thematic emphasis

## Notes on Technique Numbering

The 命书 uses original numbering from the source material. Some numbers have multiple parts but share the same technique number. Maintain original numbering for authenticity and traceability.
